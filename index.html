Scutellaria Barbata (Barbed Skullcap)

Also known as the barbed skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Asia that is part of the mint family, Lamiaceae. The root and above-ground parts of the plant are used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat bacterial infections, hepatitis, tumors, and cancer.

Bioactive Compounds:

  • Flavonoids: Have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer properties
  • Essential oils: Have antimicrobial activity
  • Polysaccharides: Have inhibited the growth of human lung cancer cells
  • Diterpenoid alkaloids: Have cytotoxic activity on cancer cell lines

Scutellaria barbata is generally up to 35 centimeters tall, with lightly toothed leaves that are somewhat lance-shaped or triangular and up to about 3 centimeters long. The flowers are borne on pedicels that have tiny, sharp bracteoles, and the purple-blue, lightly hairy flower corolla is roughly a centimeter long. The plant grows in moist and wet habitat, such as paddy fields.
